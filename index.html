Bowling Drills (Wed/Fri Sessions)

From Cricket SA drill library + biomechanics research. Rent nets for these sessions.

1

Line & Length

Target: 1m × 30cm area on good length. 6 overs of 6 balls. Start half-pace, build to full pace. Track accuracy %.

2

Seam Control (Aggot Drill)

Hold ball with seam upright, bowl at single stump. Focus on landing seam on the pitch. 4 overs. Count seam-up deliveries.

3

Red/White Ball Drill

Alternate red and white balls each delivery. Forces focus on grip adjustment between balls. 6 overs total.

4

Using the Crease

Bowl from wide of crease, then tight to stumps. Vary angles to create different lines. 4 overs each position.

5

Yorker Practice

Mark 50cm strip at batting crease. 6 overs targeting the crease line. Track hit percentage.

6

Bouncer Practice

Cross-seam, targeting above waist. Focus on back-of-a-length consistency. 4 overs.

7

Fatigued Bowling

10× run-ups (no ball) then immediately bowl 1 over. Repeat 4–6 times. Tests technique under match fatigue.

8

Match Simulation

2 batsmen in, 4-over spell. Bowl as if in a match — set fields mentally, execute plans under pressure.

The 10 Commandments
  1. Easy means EASY. If you can't hold a conversation, slow down.
  2. Never skip warm-up or cool-down. At your weight, these prevent injury.
  3. Recovery weeks are sacred. Do NOT add sessions because you "feel good."
  4. Pain above 4/10 = stop running. Switch to cycling, swimming, or elliptical.
  5. The long run is king. If you can only do one run, make it the long run.
  6. Sleep 7–8 hours minimum. The most underrated performance enhancer.
  7. Weigh yourself weekly. Same day, morning, after bathroom. Trust the trend.
  8. Practice race nutrition in training. Race day = ZERO surprises.
  9. Mumbai heat = flexibility. Treadmill or early AM when it's 38°C.
  10. Trust the process. Consistency over months creates transformation.
